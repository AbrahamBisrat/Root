What You Own (Equity)

Margin Equity

Total Securities Value - Margin Debt

This is your true liquidation value. If you sold every stock you own and paid off your margin loan immediately, this is the cash that would land in your pocket.

2

Spending Power

Non-Margin Buying Power

Essentially your "Cash on Hand".

The amount of securities you can buy without borrowing a single cent. Using this does not accrue interest.

Avail. Without Margin Impact

Liquidity without new debt.

The maximum amount you can buy without creating a NEW margin debit balance. If you stay under this, you are trading with your own money.

Margin Buying Power

Standard Limit (2x Equity)

The maximum amount you can buy and hold overnight. Under Reg T, you can borrow 50% of the purchase price.

Intraday Buying Power

Day Trading Limit (4x Equity)

Only for Pattern Day Traders. You must close these positions before market close. Holding 4x leverage overnight triggers an immediate call.

3

Safety Buffers

Net SMA (Special Memorandum Account)

The "Line of Credit" that locks in gains.

SMA is a bookkeeping entry, not cash. If your stock goes up, your SMA goes up. Crucially, if the stock drops later, SMA does NOT drop. It preserves your buying power based on your account's high-water mark.

Net House Surplus

Equity - Fidelity Requirements.
Fidelity is stricter than the government. If this number is positive, you are safe. If negative, Fidelity can liquidate assets immediately.

Net Exchange Surplus

Equity - NYSE Requirements (25%).
The absolute minimum equity required by the stock exchange. Usually lower than the House Surplus.

The Danger Zone: Margin Calls

Call Type The Trigger The Consequence
Fed Call (Reg T) Buying more than 50% on margin in a single trade. Deposit cash or sell securities within T+2 days.
House Call Equity drops below Fidelity's % requirement (30-35%). Immediate liquidation possible without notice.
Day Trade Call Exceeding Intraday Power (4x). Restricted to 2x buying power until shortfall is covered.
